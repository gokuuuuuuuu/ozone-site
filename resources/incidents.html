California DMV · AV Collision Reports

United States · California · 2014 – 2025 · CVC §38750 mandate · 913 records
State regulator·ADS test fleet·OL-316 form

The primary state-level source of ADS collision reports in the United States. Under California Vehicle Code §38750, every manufacturer holding a DMV Autonomous Vehicle Testing or Deployment Permit must report any collision involving an autonomous test vehicle within 10 days. Ozone aggregates both digitisation phases into 913 incident records from October 2014 to December 2025 — the longest continuous state-level AV collision time series currently available. The corpus is dominated by San Francisco (644, 70.5%) and the Mountain View / Silicon Valley belt where commercial AV deployment is most concentrated.

NHTSA Standing General Order · ADS + ADAS

United States · Federal · SGO 2021-01 · ADS (Jul 2021–Feb 2026) + ADAS (Aug 2019–Feb 2026) · 7,784 records
Federal·ADS + ADAS·Monthly submissions

The federal-level crash reporting mandate for automated driving systems. SGO 2021-01 requires manufacturers and operators of SAE Level 2 ADAS or Level 3–5 ADS vehicles to report any crash occurring while automation is engaged — or within 30 seconds of disengagement — within 1 day for fatal / hospitalised / airbag / tow-away crashes and within 10 days otherwise. Ozone ingests the monthly public releases and separates the ADS and ADAS sub-corpora for direct AV-vs-ADAS safety rate estimation.
